RESCUE STRETCHER

A rescue stretcher should perform the functions of a stokes and basket type stretcher, yet it has to be much more compact, light weight, and versatile in usage.

Features:

Portability - It should be rolled in a cordura pack of size – 9 inch diameter x 36 inch length & the size of the rescue stretcher should be – 3feet x 8feet laid out flat
Material - The material used in the making of stretcher should be of low density special E-Z glide
polyethylene material, Nylon webbing, solid brass grommets & steel buckles.
Temperature Resistance - Usability temperature should be to 120F without becoming brittle.
Strength - Should have horizontal lift slings with webbing tensile strength of not less than 10,000 lbs & vertical lift slings with rope tensile strength of not less than 5,800 lbs.

Basic system components of the stretcher are:

  • Stretcher
  • Cordura Back Pack/Towing Harness
  • Vertical Lift Sling (PMI E-Z Bend 3/8” rope)
  • Tow Strap
  • Set of 4 removable webbing Straps

Weight - The weight of the rescue stretcher with the components mentioned above should not be more than 19 lbs.



SPINE SPLINT

Specification:

Spine splint is used with the Stretcher and fits inside a rolled up stretcher. It is the safest, most effective vest-type extrication and immobilization device available today.” Includes a shoulder board that prevents compression of a patient in a stretcher. Excellent immobilization of the head and cervical spine, and when used with the Stretcher, functions as a long spine board. Built to last under rugged conditions. Can be cleaned with common bleach solutions. X-ray translucent


Immobilizer Collar Head

Specification:


  • • The head immobilizer collar has to be a one piece rigid cervical spine immobilization device designed to assist the rescuer with the maintenance of neutral alignment, prevention of lateral sway and anterior – posterior flexion and extension of the cervical spine during transport and routine patient care or movement.
  • • The head immobilizer collar should be provided with the NEUTRAL ALIGNMENT POSITION to prevent damaged vertebrae or foreign bodies from rubbing and/or cutting the spinal cord. Additionally, the neutral alignment position prevents the spinal cord from being twisted or compressed causing physical and/or ischemic injury to the spinal cord. Folds completely flat, Flip chin piece, Safety buttons.
  • • The Head Immobilizer Collar should be certified according to EN 46001.
